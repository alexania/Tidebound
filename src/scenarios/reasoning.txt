STEP 1 — SETTING
Location: Hreiðarvik — a small Norse fishing village clinging to a rocky fjord inlet on the western coast of Norway, late autumn. The sea is iron-grey, the hills stripped bare, and salted wind tears through the smoke-holes of longhouses. The village smells of fish guts, wet wool, and ambition. The seed "quiet-shard-anchor" suggests something precious and sharp, something that holds fast — a reputation, perhaps, or a betrothal contract nobody wanted to honour.
Season: Autumn. The herring run is ending. Men are hauling nets for the last time before winter locks the fjord.
Weather: A low iron sky pressing fog into the valley.
Arrival location: The dock — the only sensible place a visiting official arrives by sea.
Tone (Satire): Everyone in this village is absurdly invested in social hierarchy, matrimonial alliances, and the appearance of piety. The murder is the most consequential thing that has ever happened here, and half the village is furious about it mainly because it disrupted the wedding feast preparations. Characters talk around the point. Witnesses remember things only when it suits them. The powerful insist on their dignity even while obviously lying.

STEP 2 — CRIME
Victim: Ulfhéðinn "Ulfi" Brandsson — a young fisherman of good reputation, recently betrothed to Astríðr Hallsdóttir, daughter of the wealthiest man in the village, Hallr Sigurðsson. Ulfi was handsome, charming, and deeply unsuitable by any objective measure of property or bloodline. He had no land. He was beloved by Astríðr and nobody else of importance.
Perpetrator: Hallr Sigurðsson — Astríðr's father, the wealthiest merchant in Hreiðarvik, owner of two longships and a salt warehouse. He had been pressured by his wife into accepting the betrothal — Astríðr threatened to "walk into the fjord" if denied — but Hallr never accepted it. He had secretly arranged a far more advantageous match: Astríðr to Þorvarðr Egilsson, son of a powerful chieftain two fjords over. The wedding with Ulfi was set for three days' time. Hallr panicked.
Method: Garrotting with a length of braided horsehair cord — the kind used to lash cargo in Hallr's warehouse.
Murder location: The salt warehouse (by the dock).
Body found location: The salt warehouse — body was NOT moved.
Time window: Between [time:late evening] and [time:before dawn] — the night two days before the planned wedding.
The two things the perpetrator could not avoid:

The cord. Braided horsehair lashing cord is specific to Hallr's warehouse — it's used to bundle salt sacks. It is not standard fishing cordage. The perpetrator's innocent explanation: "anyone in the village could have taken a length from the warehouse, the door is never locked." The investigator must demonstrate that a specific length was recently cut from a spool still in the warehouse.
Hallr was seen by one character near the warehouse that night. His innocent explanation: "I heard a noise and went to check." The investigator must establish the noise story is impossible — because the only witness to Hallr's presence was returning from the opposite direction and would have seen Hallr leaving the warehouse, not arriving.

STEP 4 — CLUE PLAN
CAUSE OF DEATH: Garrotting with a cord or wire
Answer options: Stabbing with a fishing knife | Blunt blow to the skull | Garrotting with a cord or wire | Drowning in the fjord | Poisoning by herb or tincture
Correct Clue 1 — inspect_location (salt_warehouse)
Condition: Investigator inspects the salt warehouse.
Observation: The furrow across the victim's neck is deep and horizontal, with dark bruising in a continuous line — no puncture, no blood pooling. Near the body, a short length of braided cord lies coiled, frayed at both ends where it was separated from something longer.
Discoverability: The body is in the warehouse. Players will inspect the crime scene.
Correct Clue 2 — talk_to_character (Brynja)
Condition: Investigator talks to Brynja at the healer_hut.
Observation: Brynja examined the body briefly at dawn before it was sealed away. She says the bruising pattern across Ulfi's throat was a closed-band mark, not the open bruising of a blow or the petechiae of drowning — she has seen drowning before, she says, and it leaves different signs in the face.
Discoverability: Lead #1 directs the player to Brynja as the person who examined the body.
Red Herring 1 — stabbing
Þormóðr, when talked to, says Ulfi had an argument with a fisherman over a knife the week before. Someone's been spreading this around. (Points at Stabbing.)
Red Herring 2 — blunt blow
Inspect the warehouse: a heavy salt cask has been knocked over near the body. (Points at Blunt blow.)
Red Herring 3 — drowning
Astríðr, when talked to, mentions that Ulfi was drunk the night before and stumbled near the dock. Someone could mistake a drowned man for a strangled one. (Points at Drowning.)

TRUE LOCATION: Where the murder actually took place
Answer options: The salt warehouse | The docks | The mead hall | The healer's hut | Behind the family longhouse
Correct Clue 1 — inspect_location (salt_warehouse)
Condition: Investigator inspects the salt warehouse.
Observation: Drag marks in the salt dust on the floor would be present if someone had been moved — but the floor shows only a single scuffed area near the body, with no trail. The body's position is compact, facing the wall.
Discoverability: Body is here; players will inspect.

Red Herring 3 — mead hall
Þorvarðr says he saw Ulfi heading toward the mead hall shortly after dusk. (Points at Mead Hall.)

TIME OF DEATH: When Ulfi was killed
Answer options: Before dusk | Shortly after dusk | In the middle of the night | Before dawn | After dawn
Correct Clue 1 — inspect_item (the_cord)
Condition: Investigator inspects the braided cord item.
Observation: The cord is stiff and the braiding has begun to set in the cold — when bent, it resists at a particular angle, the way cordage does after several hours in a frost-cold room. The salt warehouse drops to near-freezing after [time:midnight].
Discoverability: Cord is found at salt_warehouse on inspection.
Correct Clue 2 — talk_to_character (Brynja)
Observation: Brynja tells the investigator that when she examined the body at first light, the flesh around the wound had already begun to stiffen, and the body had lost warmth throughout — not merely at the extremities. She says this takes several hours in cold air.
(Together: these establish death several hours before dawn — placing it in the middle of the night, ruling out before dawn and after dawn as too late, ruling out before/shortly after dusk because the cord rigidity requires hours at near-freezing temperature which only occurs after midnight.)
Discoverability: Lead #1 sends players to Brynja.
Wrong option eliminations:

"Before dusk" — eliminated by Sigríðr's testimony: she was in the warehouse at dusk and the body was not there.
"Shortly after dusk" — eliminated by cord stiffness: insufficient time in cold air.
"Before dawn" — eliminated by Brynja: body warmth lost throughout, requiring several hours, not just one.
"After dawn" — eliminated by Sigríðr: she found the body at dawn, so it cannot postdate her discovery.

Red Herring 1 — before dusk
Inspect the dock: someone reports seeing Ulfi alive "in the afternoon." (Points at Before dusk.)
Red Herring 2 — shortly after dusk
Þormóðr says Ulfi came home from the dock and seemed fine at supper, "as the sun went down." (Points at Shortly after dusk.)
Red Herring 3 — before dawn
Astríðr says she heard footsteps outside her longhouse "in the deep dark before dawn." (Points at Before dawn.)
