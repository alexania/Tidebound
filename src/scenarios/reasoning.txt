STEP 1 — SETTING
The Kauvari are a matrilineal society built around the trade of rare pigments, dyes, and mineral compounds extracted from the coastline — not salt, but ochre, viridian clay, and crystalline frost-minerals that form only in winter. Their culture centers on trade guilds called "Weavings," each controlled by a Matriarch who holds proprietary formulas for pigment refinement passed down through female lineages.
Setting: The Kauvari trading outpost of Vel-Drassik, perched on a frost-bitten headland above a grey sea. It is deep winter. The seed "heavy-blood-frost" evokes: black ice on stone steps, frost-crystals stained rust-red from ochre dust, breath hanging in cold air like smoke. The sea is iron-grey, barely visible through freezing fog. The outpost is a cluster of stone buildings around a central dye-works, accessible by a single switchback road from the lower harbour.
Season: Winter. Weather: Freezing fog with blood-ochre frost on the stones.
Arrival location: The Harbour Dock — the investigator arrives by boat from the mainland magistracy, summoned by the local Elder-Matriarch.

STEP 2 — CRIME
Victim: Sura Vel-Mak, a senior dye-formula keeper of the Weavings — she holds the only written record of the Kauvari's proprietary "Nightbloom" pigment process, which produces a pigment worth more than gold on the southern markets.
Perpetrator: Orrath Denn, the outpost's male factor (trade agent) — technically employed by the Weavings but secretly negotiating to sell the Nightbloom formula to a rival southern trading house. Sura discovered he had copied portions of the formula from the archive and confronted him privately in the Archive Chamber.
Why: Sura was about to expose him to the Elder-Matriarch Thessa Vel. Orrath had no exit — the formula sale was days away. He shot her with a small hand-crossbow (a "casting bolt" — used in the region for pest control in the dye-stores, common enough to be unremarkable).
Method: Single crossbow bolt to the chest. Cause of death: crossbow bolt wound.
Murder location: The Archive Chamber (where formula records are kept).
Body found location: The Dye-Works floor — Orrath moved her there to suggest she was inspecting the works, died by accident, or was targeted by a worker with a grudge.
Time window: Between ten o'clock and midnight (she was seen alive at ten, found at first light).
Unavoidable traces:

The bolt was fired at close range — evidence of bolt angle and entry wound will show this (medical authority clue).
Orrath has a plausible innocent explanation: he says he went to the Archive late to check trade manifests and found nothing unusual — he claims the door was open and a lamp was burning. This is true; he was there. The investigator must prove he was there with her.
Ochre-clay residue from the Archive floor (distinctive — the Archive has a specific mineral floor coating) is found on Orrath's boots — he claims he walked through after discovering the body elsewhere, but the Archive's floor dust has a unique mineral signature identifiable by the outpost's mineral-assayer.

Not personally identified: The crossbow bolt is unmarked. The residue is location-specific, not person-specific on its own.
