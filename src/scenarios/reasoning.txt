STEP 2 — CRIME
Victim: Eredan Volke, the community's Bone-Speaker (high priest of ancestor veneration). Male, 60s, gaunt, revered. Found dead at the base of the Spire of Moored Voices — fallen from the top of the sea-stack where the ancestor pillars stand.
Perpetrator: Solvaine Drest, Eredan's apprentice and designated successor. Female, 30s. Deeply committed to the old faith.
Why: Eredan had been secretly composing a theological text arguing that the ancestor pillars should be moved inland — that the sea's encroachment was a sign the ancestors wished to relocate, and that the community should follow. To Solvaine, this was not just wrong but a cosmic betrayal — the Orrish believe the ancestors are literally anchored to the place where their bones rest. Moving the pillars would "unmoor" the ancestors, condemning them and all living descendants. Solvaine killed Eredan out of genuine religious conviction: she could not allow him to enact this heresy.
Method: During the pre-dawn ancestor observance — a ritual only Solvaine and Eredan perform together — Solvaine pushed Eredan from the top of the Spire. The fall killed him. Body not moved.
Time window: Between [time:second bell and dawn] (roughly 2–4 hours before sunrise).
Unavoidable traces:

The ancestor observance is a two-person ritual — only Solvaine could have been present. But she claims Eredan sent her away early, saying he wished to commune alone. This is her innocent explanation. The investigator must find a witness who saw the two of them ascending together and no one descending alone before the body was found.
Eredan's satchel, left at the top of the Spire, contains his heretical manuscript. Solvaine claims she knew nothing of it. But the manuscript has a page with notes in two handwritings — Eredan's and Solvaine's own — showing she had read and annotated it. This is the ideological motive, and disproves her claim of ignorance.
