STEP 1 — SETTING
Location: Trevannick Cove, a remote hamlet on the north Cornish coast — a scatter of stone cottages clinging to a cliff above a narrow shingle beach, accessible by a single track road that turns treacherous in wet weather. The community has lived for generations by wrecking: lighting false signals to lure ships onto the rocks, then claiming the cargo. Everyone knows it. Nobody speaks of it.
Season: Winter. Late January.
Weather: Iron frost and a cutting easterly — the kind of cold that turns sea-spray to rime on the harbour wall.
Arrival location: The Anchor & Tallow Inn (the only public house, where the local constable has set up a temporary post and awaits the investigator).
Atmosphere (seed: narrow-frost-thorn): The cove sits in a narrow cleft in the cliffs, so the wind funnels through like a blade. Frost stiffens the seaweed on the rocks. The hawthorn hedges inland are leafless and black, all thorns. This is a place that does not welcome strangers.

STEP 2 — CRIME
Victim: Josiah Carne, 62, formerly the acknowledged "captain" of the local wrecking gang — the man who gave the signal, decided who got what share of salvage, and enforced order with violence when necessary. Retired now in name only; still collects his share from younger men out of fear and habit.
Perpetrator: Merryn Trevithick, 48, the innkeeper's wife. Her maiden name was Merryn Polglase. Twenty-two years ago, her brother Thomas Polglase drowned when a wrecking operation went wrong — Josiah, wanting a larger share and suspecting Thomas of skimming, deliberately withheld the signal lantern that would have told Thomas it was safe to swim back from the wreck. Thomas drowned. Josiah told everyone Thomas panicked and was taken by the current. Merryn knew — she had been watching from the cliff path. She has nursed this for over two decades.
Method: Poison by ingestion. Merryn prepared Josiah's customary evening cup of spiced rum toddy at the inn — she added powdered foxglove (digitalis), obtained from dried foxglove seed heads she had been collecting and grinding over several months. Foxglove grows wild on the clifftops and is common knowledge as a plant, though its deadly properties when concentrated are less widely known.
Murder location: The snug of the Anchor & Tallow Inn (a private back room where Josiah habitually drank alone after the main bar closed).
Body found location: The inn snug — same location. Body was NOT moved.
Time of death: Between [time:ten o'clock] and [time:midnight] on the night before the investigator's arrival.
The perpetrator could not avoid:

She prepared and served the drink — the cup was made for him specifically (no one else drinks spiced rum toddy at the inn; it was made fresh that evening).
She had access to the foxglove — her drying shed behind the inn contains the remains of dried foxglove seed heads, and the grinding stone has yellowish dust on it.

Plausible innocent explanation: Merryn will say she always prepares Josiah's toddy — it is a nightly habit, means nothing. The foxglove in the shed is dried for "herb bundles" she makes and sells; many coastal women dry hedgerow plants. She will not volunteer that she knows about digitalis toxicity.
What the investigator must disprove: The innocent explanation for the foxglove. A local herbalist (Widow Nancarrow) can confirm that the seed heads — not the flowers — are the most concentrated toxic part, and that grinding them defeats any notion of decorative use. The combination of the ground seed heads AND the customised cup is conclusive.
